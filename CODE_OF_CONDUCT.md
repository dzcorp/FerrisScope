# Code of Conduct

FerrisScope is an open-source project that welcomes contributors of every background and experience level. To keep the community productive and friendly, we ask everyone who participates — in issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other project space — to follow a simple set of expectations.

## Our standards

We expect all participants to:

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not with people. Critique code, not contributors.
- **Be welcoming.** Assume good faith. Help newcomers get unstuck.
- **Be patient.** Maintainers and contributors do this in their own time. A reply may take days.
- **Be clear.** Stick to facts and reproducible details when reporting bugs or reviewing changes.
- **Stay on topic.** Project spaces are for the project. Off-topic discussions belong elsewhere.

We do **not** accept:

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments directed at other participants.
- Discrimination on the basis of identity, background, or experience level.
- Sustained disruption of issues, pull requests, or discussions.
- Publishing other people's private information without their consent.
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would find inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in every space the project owns or coordinates: the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, release announcements, and any chat or call organised under the FerrisScope name. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in a public space.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates these expectations, contact the maintainers privately at **yzhelezko@gmail.com**. Reports will be:

- Acknowledged within a few days.
- Treated confidentially — only the people who need to act on the report will see it.
- Taken seriously regardless of who is involved.

You can also report concerns through GitHub's own abuse-reporting tools if you would prefer to bypass the maintainers entirely.

## Enforcement

The maintainers are responsible for clarifying these standards and will take fair, proportionate action in response to any reported violation. Possible responses include, but are not limited to:

- A private clarification of the expectations.
- A public correction or request for an apology.
- A temporary or permanent removal of access to project spaces.

Maintainers who fail to follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face the same consequences as any other participant.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct draws on the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) (version 2.1) but is written in our own words to fit the project. If you are familiar with the Contributor Covenant, the expectations here will feel familiar.
